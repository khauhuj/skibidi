My Journey Toward Becoming a Semiconductor Engineer

Published: December 2025 — 6–7 min read

The semiconductor industry is a cornerstone of modern technology and plays a critical role in shaping today’s digital society. From smartphones and computers to electric vehicles and advanced medical equipment, semiconductor devices are embedded in nearly every aspect of daily life. Choosing this field has therefore been a meaningful and deliberate step in my academic journey.

My interest in semiconductor engineering began with a curiosity about how small electronic components can perform complex tasks. Learning about transistors, integrated circuits, and microprocessors helped me realize how powerful and precise modern engineering has become. The idea that billions of transistors can operate together on a single chip continues to motivate me to explore this discipline further.

Currently, I am studying Semiconductor Engineering at Asia University’s Ho Chi Minh City campus. My coursework has provided me with a solid foundation in both theoretical knowledge and practical understanding. Through classes and independent study, I have been introduced to semiconductor fundamentals, electronic components, and basic system design. These experiences have strengthened my analytical thinking and problem-solving skills.

In addition to technical knowledge, my academic environment has emphasized the importance of communication and collaboration. Engineering is not only about individual ability, but also about working effectively in teams, sharing ideas, and presenting solutions clearly. Developing these soft skills has been an important part of my growth as a student.

Looking ahead, I plan to continue my studies in Taiwan to pursue more advanced coursework and gain exposure to international academic and industry environments. Taiwan is globally recognized for its leadership in semiconductor manufacturing and research, and studying there will allow me to learn from a highly developed ecosystem. I believe this experience will significantly enhance both my technical competence and professional perspective.

I am aware that becoming a semiconductor engineer requires sustained effort, discipline, and continuous learning. The field evolves rapidly, and engineers must constantly adapt to new technologies and methods. Despite these challenges, I remain motivated and committed to developing the skills and mindset necessary to contribute meaningfully to the semiconductor industry in the future.

This journey is still at an early stage, but it represents an important foundation for my long-term goals. Through focused study, practical experience, and international exposure, I aim to grow into a capable and responsible engineer who can contribute to technological innovation and society.
